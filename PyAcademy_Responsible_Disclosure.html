Responsible Disclosure Policy

Effective Date: May 4, 2026

The security of our systems and user data is PyAcademy's top priority. We appreciate the work of security researchers acting in good faith in identifying and reporting potential vulnerabilities. This policy provides comprehensive guidance on when and how data may be disclosed, the circumstances under which we share information with authorities, affected users, and the public, and the legal frameworks that govern these obligations.

Purpose: At PyAcademy, our mission is to help learners master Python through high-quality education while ensuring integrity and security of our platform. We've established this responsible disclosure program to collaborate with security researchers who help identify potential vulnerabilities in our systems. If you discover a vulnerability that affects multiple learning platforms, please submit separate reports to each affected organization.

Part I: Vulnerability Disclosure Program

1.1 Scope of Systems

This Policy covers all internet-facing information systems, applications, or websites owned, operated, or controlled by PyAcademy, including any web or mobile applications hosted on li1234.top and related subdomains (collectively, "Information Systems"). It does not cover third-party systems even if under a PyAcademy domain.

1.2 Scope of Vulnerabilities

This Policy covers technical vulnerabilities such as misconfigurations, CSRF, privilege escalation, SQLi, XSS, directory traversal. Exclusions (at PyAcademy's discretion) include: missing security best practices without PoC, rate limiting on non-auth endpoints, account takeover via brute force, social engineering, denial of service, clickjacking without sensitive actions, and widely publicized zero-days with patch available less than 30 days. For safety/jailbreak issues, report security@li1234.topre timing, not engage in social engineering, comply with laws. PyAcademy will acknowledge within 3 business days and strive to resolve promptly.

1.4 Reporting Requirements in Detail

Each vulnerability report must include the following information to enable efficient triage and remediation:

  • Vulnerability Summary: A concise description of the vulnerability type and its location within the system.
  • Steps to Reproduce: Clear, step-by-step instructions that allow our engineering team to reproduce the issue reliably.
  • Affected URLs / Endpoints: The exact URLs, API endpoints, or page paths where the vulnerability exists.
  • Proof of Concept (PoC): Functional exploit code, HTTP request/response pairs, or screenshots demonstrating the vulnerability. Redact any personal data of other users before submission.
  • Impact Assessment: A description of the potential impact, including what data could be accessed, modified, or deleted, and whether the vulnerability could be chained with other issues.
  • Remediation Suggestions: Recommendations for fixing the vulnerability, if available. Where possible, reference relevant OWASP guidance or specific technical standards.
  • Environment Details: Browser version, operating system, and any relevant tools or configurations used during testing.
Important: Reports should be written in clear, simple terms. Our initial triage team includes developers who may not have specialized security backgrounds. Avoid unnecessary jargon and provide sufficient context for each finding.

1.5 Disclosure Timeline and Process

PyAcademy follows a Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD) model, which balances the need for timely public awareness with the need for organizations to develop and deploy fixes:

  • Day 0 – Initial Report: Researcher submits the vulnerability report via our designated channel.
  • Within 3 Business Days – Acknowledgment: PyAcademy confirms receipt and assigns an internal tracking identifier.
  • Within 10 Business Days – Initial Assessment: PyAcademy provides an initial severity assessment (using CVSS v3.1 scoring) and a preliminary remediation timeline.
  • Ongoing – Status Updates: Regular updates are provided to the researcher at least every 14 days during active remediation.
  • Upon Patch Release – Coordinated Disclosure: Once a fix is deployed, PyAcademy and the researcher coordinate public disclosure, including publishing a security advisory and requesting a CVE identifier where appropriate.
  • 90-Day Maximum: Following the approach of Google Project Zero, if no patch has been released within 90 days of the initial report, the researcher may proceed with public disclosure. PyAcademy will make every effort to resolve the issue before this deadline.

1.6 What You May and May Not Do

Permitted Activities:

  • Test only against systems explicitly listed in the Scope of Systems section above.
  • Use the minimum level of exploitation necessary to demonstrate that a vulnerability exists.
  • Access or interact only with your own test accounts or data explicitly created for testing purposes.
  • Stop testing immediately upon discovering a vulnerability and report it promptly.
  • If you encounter sensitive data (personally identifiable information, financial data, credentials, or proprietary information of any party), you must stop your test immediately, not store or exfiltrate any data, and notify PyAcademy right away.

Prohibited Activities:

  • Do not use exploits to compromise or exfiltrate data, establish persistent access, or pivot to other systems.
  • Do not conduct denial-of-service (DoS/DDoS) attacks, including automated high-volume requests.
  • Do not engage in social engineering attacks against PyAcademy employees, contractors, or users.
  • Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users.
  • Do not submit a high volume of low-quality or automated reports.
  • Do not demand payment or rewards as a condition for disclosing vulnerability details or refraining from public disclosure — this may constitute extortion.
  • Do not use automated vulnerability scanners without prior authorization.

1.7 Safe Harbor

If you make a good faith effort to research and disclose vulnerabilities in accordance with this Policy, PyAcademy will not pursue legal action against you. Safe Harbor is conditional on unconditional disclosure without extortion or threats. This protection applies provided that you:

  • Stay within the defined scope and rules of this program at all times.
  • Do not access, store, or exfiltrate any personal data, financial information, or proprietary data beyond the minimum necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability.
  • Do not share vulnerability details with any third party before the coordinated disclosure date.
  • Comply with all applicable laws and regulations in your jurisdiction.
Legal Disclaimer: This Policy does not constitute legal advice. Security research laws vary by jurisdiction. Researchers are responsible for understanding and complying with the laws applicable in their location. If in doubt, consult a qualified legal professional before conducting any security testing.

1.8 Reward and Recognition

PyAcademy currently does not operate a formal bug bounty program with monetary rewards. However, we are committed to recognizing researchers who contribute to our security:

  • Public Credit: With the researcher's permission, we will credit them in our security advisories and on our security hall of fame page.
  • CVE Coordination: We will request CVE identifiers for qualifying vulnerabilities and attribute them to the discoverer.
  • Souvenirs: We may offer PyAcademy branded merchandise (stickers, t-shirts) as a token of appreciation at our discretion.

If PyAcademy launches a formal bug bounty program in the future, details will be published on our website and this Policy will be updated accordingly.

Part II: Data Breach Disclosure – When and How PyAcademy Discloses Data

This section describes the circumstances under which PyAcademy may disclose or share data, including personal data, with external parties such as regulatory authorities, affected users, law enforcement, and the public. It also outlines the legal frameworks that govern these obligations.

2.1 Overview of Disclosure Circumstances

PyAcademy may disclose data in the following categories of circumstances:

  • Regulatory and Legal Obligations: When required by applicable data protection laws, cybersecurity regulations, or government authorities.
  • Data Breach Notifications: When a security incident results in the unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or loss of personal data.
  • Law Enforcement Requests: When compelled by valid legal process, such as court orders, subpoenas, or warrants.
  • Protection of Rights and Safety: When necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of PyAcademy, our users, or the public.
  • Business Transfers: In connection with mergers, acquisitions, asset sales, or other corporate restructuring.
  • De-identified and Aggregated Data: Anonymized or aggregated data that cannot reasonably be linked to individual users.

2.2 Data Breach Notification – Regulatory Frameworks

PyAcademy operates under multiple legal frameworks that impose data breach notification obligations. The applicable framework depends on the location and nationality of the affected individuals:

2.2.1 General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – European Union

For data breaches affecting individuals in the European Economic Area (EEA), PyAcademy complies with GDPR Articles 33 and 34:

  • Notification to Supervisory Authority (Article 33): PyAcademy shall notify the relevant Data Protection Authority (DPA) without undue delay and, where feasible, no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of a personal data breach, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals. If the 72-hour deadline cannot be met, the notification shall include the reasons for the delay and be submitted in phases as information becomes available.
  • Notification to Affected Individuals (Article 34): When the breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, PyAcademy shall communicate the breach to the affected data subjects without undue delay. This higher threshold applies when the breach could lead to identity theft, financial loss, discrimination, reputational damage, or other significant harm.
  • Exemptions from Individual Notification: Individual notification may not be required if: (a) appropriate technical and organizational protection measures were applied that render the data unintelligible (e.g., encryption); (b) subsequent measures ensure that the high risk is no longer likely to materialize; or (c) individual notification would require disproportionate effort, in which case public communication may be used instead.

2.2.2 People's Republic of China – Cybersecurity Law, Data Security Law, and Personal Information Protection Law

For data breaches affecting individuals within the People's Republic of China, PyAcademy complies with the following requirements:

  • Cybersecurity Law (Article 25): Network operators shall immediately remediate security vulnerabilities, risks, and other hazards. In the event of a data breach or security incident that results in the leakage, damage, or loss of personal data, the operator shall immediately notify the affected users, report the incident to the relevant competent authorities, and take necessary remedial measures.
  • Personal Information Protection Law (Article 57): In the event of a personal information security incident, the personal information handler shall immediately take remedial measures, notify the affected individuals, and report to the competent department. The notification to individuals shall include: the type and cause of the security incident, the categories of personal information that have been or may have been leaked, the potential impact and harm to the individuals, the remedial measures already taken, the measures that individuals can take to reduce harm, and the contact information of the personal information handler.
  • Data Security Regulation (effective January 1, 2025): Network data processors shall establish emergency response plans for network data security incidents. When an incident occurs, the processor shall immediately activate the plan, take measures to prevent the expansion of harm, eliminate safety hazards, and report to the relevant competent authorities. When a network data security incident causes harm to the lawful rights and interests of individuals or organizations, the processor shall promptly notify the affected stakeholders of the incident details, risk situation, consequences, and remedial measures already taken, via telephone, SMS, instant messaging, email, or public announcement.
  • 24-Hour Reporting for Critical Incidents: Where a security incident involves potential harm to national security or public interest, PyAcademy shall report to the relevant competent authorities within 24 hours of becoming aware of the incident.

2.2.3 Other Jurisdictions

PyAcademy also monitors and complies with data breach notification requirements in other jurisdictions where our users are located, including but not limited to:

  • United States: State-level breach notification laws (e.g., California CCPA/CPRA, New York SHIELD Act) which typically require notification within 30–60 days of discovery, with specific content requirements varying by state.
  • United Kingdom: UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, requiring notification to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours where the breach poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms.
  • ISO 27001 (Annex A.5.5 – Contact with Authorities): PyAcademy maintains documented procedures for contacting relevant authorities, including data protection authorities, regulatory bodies, and law enforcement, as part of our information security management system.

2.3 How PyAcademy Discloses Data – Detailed Procedures

2.3.1 Internal Incident Response Workflow

Upon detection or notification of a potential data breach, PyAcademy follows a structured internal workflow:

  • Step 1 – Detection and Intake: The incident is logged in our incident tracking system. The security team confirms receipt and begins an initial investigation within 4 hours.
  • Step 2 – Containment: Immediate measures are taken to contain the incident and prevent further data loss, including isolating affected systems, revoking compromised credentials, and blocking malicious access.
  • Step 3 – Investigation and Assessment: The security team conducts a thorough investigation to determine: the nature and scope of the breach; the categories and approximate number of individuals affected; the categories of data involved; the likely consequences and risk level; and whether the data was encrypted or otherwise protected.
  • Step 4 – Risk Classification: The breach is classified into one of three risk tiers based on severity and potential impact:
    • Tier 1 – Low Risk: The breach is unlikely to result in any risk to individuals. No external notification is required beyond internal documentation.
    • Tier 2 – Risk to Rights and Freedoms: The breach may result in risk to individuals. Notification to the relevant supervisory authority is required within 72 hours.
    • Tier 3 – High Risk: The breach is likely to result in a high risk to individuals (e.g., identity theft, financial loss, discrimination). Direct notification to affected individuals is required without undue delay, in addition to supervisory authority notification.
  • Step 5 – Notification Execution: Based on the risk classification, PyAcademy prepares and delivers notifications to the appropriate parties (see Section 2.3.2 for notification content).
  • Step 6 – Remediation and Recovery: The engineering team develops, tests, and deploys fixes. Affected systems are restored from clean backups where necessary. Additional security controls are implemented to prevent recurrence.
  • Step 7 – Post-Incident Review: A comprehensive post-incident review is conducted within 30 days to identify lessons learned, update security controls, and refine incident response procedures.

2.3.2 Notification Content Requirements

When PyAcademy is required to notify supervisory authorities or affected individuals, the notification shall include, to the extent available at the time of notification:

  • The nature of the breach, including where possible the categories and approximate number of individuals and data records affected.
  • The name and contact details of PyAcademy's data protection officer or other contact point from whom more information can be obtained.
  • The likely consequences of the breach for affected individuals.
  • The measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.
  • Specific guidance for affected individuals on steps they can take to protect themselves (e.g., changing passwords, monitoring financial accounts, enabling two-factor authentication).

If the full scope of the breach is not yet known at the time of initial notification, PyAcademy will provide updates as information becomes available, in phases if necessary.

2.3.3 Notification Methods

PyAcademy uses the following methods to deliver notifications, depending on the nature and severity of the incident:

  • Email: Direct email notification to affected registered users at the email address associated with their account.
  • Platform Notification: In-app or on-site notification banners and alerts displayed upon user login.
  • Public Announcement: For large-scale incidents, a public security advisory published on our website and communicated through our official channels.
  • SMS / Instant Messaging: For critical incidents requiring immediate user action, PyAcademy may send SMS or instant messaging notifications where contact information is available.
  • Press Statement: In cases of significant public interest, PyAcademy may issue a press statement through recognized media channels.

2.4 Disclosure to Law Enforcement and Government Authorities

PyAcademy may disclose personal data or other information to law enforcement agencies, government authorities, or regulatory bodies in the following circumstances:

  • Valid Legal Process: In response to a legally binding request, such as a court order, subpoena, search warrant, or other compulsory legal process issued by a competent authority.
  • Emergency Disclosure: When PyAcademy reasonably believes that disclosure is necessary to prevent imminent and serious harm to the life, health, or safety of any individual, or to prevent a serious crime.
  • National Security Requirements: When required by applicable national security laws or regulations, including lawful interception requirements.
  • Criminal Investigation Cooperation: When PyAcademy discovers evidence of criminal activity during the course of a security incident investigation, we will report to the relevant law enforcement agencies (e.g., police, national security agencies) and cooperate with their investigation.
Transparency Principle: Whenever legally permissible, PyAcademy will notify affected users before disclosing their data to law enforcement, unless doing so would impede an investigation or create a risk of harm. We publish an annual transparency report summarizing the number and types of government and law enforcement requests we receive.

2.5 Disclosure for Protection of Rights and Safety

PyAcademy may disclose limited user data in the following circumstances to protect rights and safety:

  • To Protect Our Users: To investigate and prevent fraud, unauthorized access, abuse, or other violations of our Terms of Service that may harm other users.
  • To Protect PyAcademy: To enforce our legal rights, defend against legal claims, or investigate potential violations of our policies.
  • To Protect the Public: To address technical vulnerabilities that could affect other organizations or the broader internet community, in accordance with coordinated disclosure principles.
  • To Prevent Harm: When we reasonably believe that a user's actions pose a threat to the safety of others or themselves.

2.6 Disclosure in Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of all or a portion of PyAcademy's assets, user data may be transferred to the acquiring entity as part of the transaction. In such cases:

  • We will notify affected users via email or a prominent site notice before the transfer of their personal data to a different entity becomes effective.
  • The acquiring entity will assume the obligations of this Policy and all applicable privacy commitments.
  • Users will be provided with the opportunity to exercise their rights regarding their personal data, including the right to delete their account and associated data before the transfer takes effect.
  • We will use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the transferee provides an adequate level of data protection.

2.7 De-identified and Aggregated Data

PyAcademy may collect, use, and disclose de-identified or aggregated data that cannot reasonably be used to identify individual users. This includes:

  • Statistical data about platform usage, learning patterns, and course completion rates.
  • Aggregated demographic information for research and reporting purposes.
  • Anonymized security metrics for industry benchmarking and threat intelligence sharing.

Once data is truly de-identified and cannot be re-linked to individuals, it is no longer considered personal data under applicable privacy laws and may be freely used and disclosed.

Part III: Security Advisory Publication

3.1 When PyAcademy Publishes Security Advisories

PyAcademy publishes security advisories in the following circumstances:

  • When a vulnerability in our systems has been remediated and a patch or fix has been deployed.
  • When a vulnerability in a third-party dependency used by our platform has been addressed.
  • When a data breach or security incident has occurred that affects our users, and we have completed our initial investigation.
  • When we become aware of industry-wide vulnerabilities or threats that may affect our users.

3.2 Content of Security Advisories

Published security advisories will include, where applicable:

  • A high-level summary of the vulnerability or incident and its impact.
  • The affected versions, components, or services, and the versions in which the issue has been resolved.
  • Technical details of the vulnerability, including CVSS severity score and CWE classification.
  • Mitigations and workarounds for users who cannot immediately apply the fix.
  • Links to the vendor's published advisory (for third-party components).
  • A timeline of discovery, internal communication, and remediation.
  • Acknowledgment of the security researcher who reported the issue (with their explicit permission).
  • CVE identifiers where applicable.

3.3 Publication Timeline

Security advisories are published according to the following timeline:

  • Coordinated Disclosure: Advisories are published simultaneously with or shortly after the deployment of the fix, in coordination with the reporting researcher.
  • Third-Party Vulnerabilities: Advisories are published within 24 hours of the patch being deployed to our production environment.
  • Data Breach Notifications: Public advisories are published as soon as practicable after affected individuals and relevant authorities have been notified, and in compliance with all applicable legal requirements.

Part IV: User Rights Regarding Data Disclosure

4.1 Rights of Data Subjects

PyAcademy recognizes and respects the following rights of individuals whose data we process:

  • Right of Access: You have the right to request a copy of the personal data PyAcademy holds about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Erasure: You have the right to request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain legal exceptions (e.g., legal obligations, legitimate business interests).
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You have the right to request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to certain types of processing, including processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

Part V: Compliance and Governance

5.1 Data Protection Impact Assessments

PyAcademy conducts Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) before undertaking any processing activity that is likely to result in a high risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, including:

  • Systematic and extensive profiling with significant effects.
  • Large-scale processing of sensitive personal data.
  • Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas.
  • Use of new technologies that may pose heightened privacy risks.

5.2 Regular Audits and Reviews

PyAcademy periodically conducts internal and external audits of its data protection and security practices, including:

  • Annual compliance audits against GDPR, PIPL, and other applicable regulations.
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments of our systems.
  • Quarterly reviews of access controls and data handling procedures.
  • Annual training for all employees on data protection and security awareness.

5.3 Data Retention and Secure Disposal

PyAcademy retains personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymized using industry-standard methods.

5.4 Subprocessor and Third-Party Disclosure

PyAcademy may share personal data with trusted third-party service providers (subprocessors) who assist us in operating our platform, such as cloud hosting providers, payment processors, and analytics services. All subprocessors are bound by contractual obligations to protect personal data to at least the same standard as required by this Policy. A current list of subprocessors is available upon request.

Changes to This Policy

PyAcademy may update this Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legal requirements, industry best practices, or our operational practices. When we make material changes, we will notify users by posting the updated Policy on our website and, where appropriate, by sending an email notification. 第二部分：数据泄露披露 – PyAcademy 在何种情况下披露数据

2.1 披露情形概览

PyAcademy 可能在以下类别的情形下披露数据：

  • 监管和法律义务：当适用数据保护法律、网络安全法规或政府主管部门要求时。
  • 数据泄露通知：当安全事件导致个人数据被未授权访问、披露、更改或丢失时。

2.2 数据泄露通知 – 法律框架

PyAcademy 受多个法律框架的约束，这些框架规定了数据泄露通知义务。适用的框架取决于受影响个人的所在地和国籍：

2.2.1 欧盟《通用数据保护条例》（GDPR）

对于影响欧洲经济区（EEA）内个人的数据泄露，PyAcademy 遵守 GDPR 第33条和第34条：

  • 向监管机构通知（第33条）：PyAcademy 应在知悉个人数据泄露后，无不合理迟延地、且在可行的情况下不迟于72小时内向相关数据保护机构（DPA）发出通知，除非该泄露不太可能对个人的权利和自由造成风险。如果无法在72小时期限内完成通知，应在信息可用时分阶段提交，并说明延迟原因。
  • 向受影响个人通知（第34条）：当泄露可能对个人的权利和自由造成高风险时，PyAcademy 应无不合理迟延地将泄露情况告知受影响的数据主体。此更高门槛适用于泄露可能导致身份盗用、经济损失、歧视、声誉损害或其他重大损害的情形。
  • 个人通知的豁免情形：在以下情况下可能无需向个人通知：(a) 已采取适当的技术和组织保护措施使数据不可识别（如加密）；(b) 后续措施确保高风险不再可能出现；(c) 个人通知需要不成比例的努力，此时可使用公开通知代替。

2.2.2 中华人民共和国 – 网络安全法、数据安全法和个人信息保护法

对于影响中华人民共和国境内个人的数据泄露，PyAcademy 遵守以下要求：

  • 《网络安全法》第25条：网络运营者应当立即修复安全漏洞、风险和其他隐患。发生数据泄露或安全事件导致个人信息泄露、损毁、丢失的，应当立即通知受影响用户，向有关主管部门报告，并采取必要的补救措施。
  • 《个人信息保护法》第57条：发生个人信息安全事件时，个人信息处理者应当立即采取补救措施，通知受影响的个人，并向主管部门报告。向个人发出的通知应包括：安全事件的类型和原因、已经或可能泄露的个人信息类别、对个人可能造成的影响和危害、已采取的补救措施、个人可以采取的减轻危害的措施，以及个人信息处理者的联系方式。
  • 《网络数据安全管理条例》（2025年1月1日起施行）：网络数据处理者应当建立健全网络数据安全事件应急预案，发生事件时应立即启动预案，采取措施防止危害扩大、消除安全隐患，并按规定向有关主管部门报告。当网络数据安全事件对个人、组织合法权益造成危害时，应当及时以电话、短信、即时通信工具、电子邮件或公告等方式将安全事件和风险情况、危害后果、已采取的补救措施等通知利害关系人。
  • 涉及国家安全和公共利益的24小时报告：当安全事件可能危害国家安全或公共利益时，PyAcademy 应在知悉事件后24小时内向有关主管部门报告。

2.2.3 其他司法管辖区

PyAcademy 还持续关注并遵守用户所在其他司法管辖区的数据泄露通知要求，包括但不限于：

  • 美国：各州层面的泄露通知法律（如加州 CCPA/CPRA、纽约 SHIELD 法案），通常要求在发现后30–60天内通知，具体内容要求因州而异。
  • 英国：英国 GDPR 和《2018年数据保护法》，要求在泄露对个人权利和自由构成风险时，在72小时内通知信息专员办公室（ICO）。
  • ISO 27001（附录 A.5.5 – 与当局的联系）：作为我们信息安全管理体系的一部分，PyAcademy 保留了与相关机构（包括数据保护机构、监管机构和执法部门）联系的书面程序。

2.3 PyAcademy 如何披露数据 – 详细流程

2.3.1 内部事件响应工作流程

在检测到或收到潜在数据泄露通知后，PyAcademy 按照结构化的内部工作流程进行处理：

  • 第1步 – 检测和受理：事件记录在我们的事件跟踪系统中。安全团队在4小时内确认收悉并开始初步调查。
  • 第2步 – 遏制：立即采取措施遏制事件并防止进一步的数据丢失，包括隔离受影响的系统、撤销被泄露的凭据以及阻止恶意访问。
  • 第3步 – 调查和评估：安全团队进行彻底调查，确定：泄露的性质和范围；受影响的个人类别和大致数量；涉及的数据类别；可能的后果和风险等级；以及数据是否已加密或以其他方式受到保护。
  • 第4步 – 风险分级：根据严重性和潜在影响，将泄露分为三个风险等级：
    • 一级 – 低风险：泄露不太可能对个人造成任何风险。除内部记录外，无需外部通知。
    • 二级 – 对权利和自由有风险：泄露可能对个人造成风险。需要在72小时内通知相关监管机构。
    • 三级 – 高风险：泄露可能对个人造成高风险（如身份盗用、经济损失、歧视）。除监管机构通知外，还需无不合理迟延地直接通知受影响个人。
  • 第5步 – 通知执行：根据风险分级，PyAcademy 准备并向相关方发送通知（通知内容详见第2.3.2节）。
  • 第6步 – 修复和恢复：工程团队开发、测试并部署修复方案。必要时从干净备份恢复受影响的系统。实施额外的安全控制措施以防止再次发生。
  • 第7步 – 事后审查：在30天内进行全面的事后审查，总结经验教训，更新安全控制措施，并完善事件响应流程。

2.3.2 通知内容要求

当 PyAcademy 需要通知监管机构或受影响个人时，通知应在通知时已掌握的信息范围内包含以下内容：

  • 泄露的性质，包括在可能的情况下说明受影响的个人类别和大致数量以及数据记录类别。
  • PyAcademy 数据保护官或其他可获取更多信息的联系点的姓名和联系方式。
  • 泄露对受影响个人可能造成的后果。
  • 已采取或拟采取的应对泄露的措施，包括在适当时减轻其可能不利影响的措施。
  • 为受影响个人提供的具体指导，说明他们可以采取的保护措施（如更改密码、监控金融账户、启用双重身份验证）。

如果在初始通知时泄露的完整范围尚不清楚，PyAcademy 将在信息可用时提供更新，必要时分阶段进行。

2.3.3 通知方式

PyAcademy 根据事件的性质和严重程度，使用以下方式发送通知：

  • 电子邮件：向受影响的注册用户在其账户关联的邮箱地址发送直接邮件通知。
  • 平台通知：应用内或网站上的通知横幅和提醒，在用户登录时显示。
  • 公开公告：对于大规模事件，在我们的网站上发布公开安全公告，并通过官方渠道传达。
  • 短信 / 即时通信：对于需要用户立即采取行动的紧急事件，PyAcademy 可在联系方式可用时发送短信或即时通信通知。
  • 新闻声明：在涉及重大公共利益的情况下，PyAcademy 可通过认可的媒体渠道发布新闻声明。

2.4 向执法部门和政府机构披露

PyAcademy 可能在以下情况下向执法机构、政府主管部门或监管机构披露个人数据或其他信息：
