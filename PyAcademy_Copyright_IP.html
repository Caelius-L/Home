Copyright and Intellectual Property Protection Summary

Effective Date: May 4, 2026

This summary outlines PyAcademy's approach to copyright and intellectual property protection. It describes how content on our platform is owned and protected, how we handle user-generated content, and how to report potential infringement. This document is drafted in compliance with, and should be read in conjunction with, the following applicable legal instruments: the Directive (EU) 2019/790 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 17 April 2019 on copyright and related rights in the Digital Single Market ("Copyright Directive"), Directive 2001/29/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 22 May 2001 on the harmonisation of copyright and related rights in the information society ("InfoSoc Directive"), the French Intellectual Property Code (Code de la propriété intellectuelle, hereinafter "CPI"), Regulation (EU) 2017/1001 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 14 June 2017 on the European Union trade mark ("EU Trade Mark Regulation"), and the Berne Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works of 9 September 1886 (as revised). To the extent applicable, the provisions of the EU Directive 2000/31/EC on certain legal aspects of information society services, in particular electronic commerce, in the Internal Market ("E-Commerce Directive"), and the French Law No. 2004-575 of 21 June 2004 for confidence in the digital economy ("LCEN") shall also apply.

Our Principle: PyAcademy respects the intellectual property of others and expects our users to do the same. We are committed to protecting the rights of content creators while fostering a collaborative learning environment.

1. Ownership of Platform Content

All original content on PyAcademy — including but not limited to course materials, tutorials, video lessons, interactive exercises, assessments, documentation, graphics, logos, and the selection and arrangement thereof — is owned by PyAcademy or its content providers and is protected by European Union and international copyright laws.

In accordance with the French Intellectual Property Code (CPI), copyright protection attaches automatically to original works of authorship by virtue of Articles L111-1 et seq. of the CPI, which provide that the author of a work of the mind shall enjoy, by the sole fact of its creation, an exclusive and intangible property right in said work, enforceable against all persons. This protection extends to all original works regardless of genre, form of expression, merit, or purpose, as stipulated in Article L112-1 of the CPI. The moral rights of attribution and integrity, as recognised under Articles L121-1 et seq. of the CPI, are inalienable and perpetual.

Course content, learning paths, and educational materials developed by PyAcademy are the exclusive intellectual property of PyAcademy and may not be reproduced, distributed, or used for commercial purposes without prior written authorization. Such materials are further protected under Articles L122-1 et seq. of the CPI, which govern the exclusive right of reproduction, and Articles L122-1 et seq. governing the exclusive right of communication to the public.

2. Trademarks

The PyAcademy name, logo, and all related marks, designs, and slogans are trademarks of PyAcademy. These trademarks are protected under Regulation (EU) 2017/1001 (EU Trade Mark Regulation) and the French Intellectual Property Code (CPI), Articles L711-1 et seq. You may not use these marks without our prior written permission. All goodwill generated by the use of PyAcademy trademarks inures to the benefit of PyAcademy. Any unauthorised use of these trademarks may constitute infringement giving rise to civil and criminal liability under Articles L716-1 et seq. of the CPI.

4. Fair Use and Educational Exception

PyAcademy may incorporate content under fair use or educational exceptions as permitted by the Copyright Directive (2019/790), the InfoSoc Directive (2001/29/EC), and applicable national laws, including but not limited to Article L122-5 of the French Intellectual Property Code (CPI). Article L122-5 of the CPI provides that once a work has been lawfully made available to the public, the author may not prohibit reproduction of the work exclusively intended for teaching or scientific research, subject to attribution and provided that such use does not cause unnecessary prejudice to the normal exploitation of the work or to the legitimate interests of the author. Limited portions of third-party content may be used within our courses for purposes such as:

  • Criticism, commentary, and analysis of programming concepts (consistent with Article L122-5, 4° of the CPI)
  • Teaching and illustration within an educational context (consistent with Article L122-5, 6° of the CPI, as amended by the Copyright Directive transposition)
  • Reference to open-source code and libraries with proper attribution
  • Quotation in accordance with Article L122-5, 3° of the CPI, provided that it is justified by the critical, polemical, educational, scientific, or informational nature of the work in which it is incorporated

Any such use will be accompanied by appropriate attribution, will indicate the source and, unless the work is in the public domain, the name of the author and holder of the rights in the work, and will not exceed what is necessary for the educational purpose. Such exceptions shall be interpreted strictly in accordance with the three-step test as codified in Article 5(5) of the InfoSoc Directive and Article L122-5 of the CPI.

    6.2. Our Response Process

    Upon receiving a valid copyright complaint, PyAcademy will:

    1. Acknowledge receipt of your complaint within 24 hours
    2. Review the reported content and associated materials
    3. Take appropriate action, which may include removing or disabling access to the infringing content
    4. Notify the user who posted the content of the complaint and any action taken
    5. Provide the accused party with an opportunity to respond or submit a counter-notification
    6. Resolve the matter, typically within 10 business days

    6.3. Counter-Notifications

    If content was removed due to a copyright complaint and you believe the removal was mistaken or that you have authorization to use the material, you may submit a counter-notification containing:

    • Your signature (physical or electronic)
    • Identification of the removed content and its former location
    • A statement under penalty of perjury that you believe the content was removed in error or misidentification
    • Your consent to the jurisdiction of the applicable courts
    • Your contact information

    7. Repeat Infringers

    PyAcademy maintains a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of users who are determined to be repeat infringers. A repeat infringer is a user who has been notified of infringing activity and/or has had content removed from the platform more than twice. This policy is implemented in accordance with Article 18 of Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (the Digital Services Act, "DSA"), which establishes notice-and-action mechanisms requiring intermediaries to provide transparent and timely processes for handling reports of illegal content. In compliance with Article 18 DSA, PyAcademy ensures that notices submitted through its complaint mechanism are processed promptly, that decision notifications are provided to the complainant with sufficient reasons, and that internal complaint-handling mechanisms are made available for the review of moderation decisions. Furthermore, in accordance with Recital 75 and Article 14 of the DSA, PyAcademy provides clear information regarding its content moderation policies and the grounds for any restrictions imposed on users.
