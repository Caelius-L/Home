Account Penalties and Appeals Guide

Effective Date: May 4, 2026

This guide describes the penalty system PyAcademy uses to enforce our Community Guidelines, and the process you can follow if you believe a penalty was applied in error.

Legal Basis and Regulatory Framework: This penalty and appeals framework is adopted pursuant to, and shall be interpreted in accordance with, the following regulatory instruments: Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 14 October 2022 on a Single Market For Digital Services (the "Digital Services Act" or "DSA"), in particular Articles 14 through 20 thereof concerning the obligations of hosting service providers, transparency requirements, and mechanisms for notice-and-action and internal complaint-handling; Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data (the "General Data Protection Regulation" or "GDPR"), with particular regard to the principles of data processing set forth in Articles 5 and 6 and the rights of data subjects under Articles 15 through 22; the French Civil Code (Code civil), including but not limited to provisions governing good faith performance of contracts and liability in tort; and the French Consumer Code (Code de la consommation), in particular provisions relating to unfair commercial practices, consumer information rights, and the right to effective remedy. All enforcement actions taken pursuant to this guide shall comply with the fundamental rights and freedoms guaranteed by the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union, including the right to freedom of expression (Article 11) and the right to an effective remedy and a fair trial (Article 47).

1. Purpose of the Penalty System

The penalty system exists to protect the PyAcademy community. Its purpose is not to punish, but to maintain a respectful, safe, and productive learning environment for all users. Enforcement actions are proportionate to the severity and frequency of the violation, and PyAcademy aims to give users the opportunity to correct their behavior before imposing more severe penalties.

2. How Violations Are Identified

Violations may be identified through:

  • Reports from other community members using the "Report" button or email
  • Automated detection systems that flag potentially harmful content
  • Reviews conducted by PyAcademy's moderation team as part of routine platform oversight
  • Requests from law enforcement or regulatory authorities where legally required

Every flagged item is reviewed by a trained member of PyAcademy's Trust & Safety team before any action is taken.

The notice-and-action mechanisms employed by PyAcademy are established in accordance with Article 16 of the DSA, which requires hosting service providers to act in a timely, diligent, and non-arbitrary manner upon obtaining actual knowledge of, or being in possession of facts or circumstances from which it is apparent that, illegal content exists on their platform. All notices submitted by third parties are evaluated against the criteria set forth in Article 16(2) of the DSA to determine whether they contain sufficient information to enable the provider to identify the content at issue. Furthermore, in compliance with Article 20 of the DSA, PyAcademy maintains an internal complaint-handling mechanism that enables any affected recipient of a decision to contest such decisions, including decisions based on the provider's terms and conditions where they go beyond the obligations imposed by Union or national law.

3. Penalty Tiers

Penalties are applied on a graduated scale based on the nature and frequency of the violation. In accordance with the principle of proportionality enshrined in Article 14(3) of the DSA, each penalty imposed shall be tailored to the specific circumstances of the case and shall not exceed what is strictly necessary and proportionate to achieve the objective of ensuring compliance with PyAcademy's terms and conditions and applicable law. The assessment of proportionality takes into account the interests involved, the potential consequences for the user, and the possibility of achieving the same objective through less restrictive measures.

Level 1: Warning

A formal notification informing you that your behavior has violated our Community Guidelines and must stop immediately. A warning is recorded on your account but does not restrict your access to any features. Warnings are typically issued for:

  • First-time minor violations (e.g., off-topic posting, mild language issues)
  • Unintentional breaches of community standards (e.g., accidental posting of duplicate content, inadvertent misuse of formatting tools)
  • Content that requires minor adjustments to meet guidelines (e.g., posts lacking proper citation, incomplete code snippets in discussion threads)
  • Minor infractions of discussion etiquette, such as derailing a thread topic or making comments that are tangential to the course material
  • Inadvertent sharing of information that, while not harmful, does not conform to the platform's academic integrity standards
Level 2: Content Removal

The offending content is removed from the platform. You will receive a notification explaining which content was removed and why. Content removal is typically issued for:

  • Posts, comments, or submissions that violate specific community guidelines (e.g., posting content in the wrong forum section, sharing links to external resources that conflict with the platform's academic integrity policy)
  • Sharing complete assignment solutions that circumvent the learning process (e.g., posting full source code for graded exercises, providing test cases intended for personal assessment completion)
  • Content containing inappropriate, harmful, or inaccurate information (e.g., misleading technical guidance that could damage users' computing environments, content promoting unsafe coding practices)
  • Excessive self-promotion or off-topic content (e.g., unsolicited advertising of external services, repeated cross-posting of the same promotional material across multiple course forums)
  • Unauthorized redistribution of course materials, including recordings of live sessions, proprietary datasets, or supplementary learning resources distributed under a restricted license
  • Content that violates applicable intellectual property rights, including unauthorized reproduction of third-party copyrighted material
Level 3: Temporary Suspension

Restricted access to specific community features (such as forums, discussions, or course interactions) for a defined period. Full access to course content and materials is generally maintained. Temporary suspension is typically issued for:

  • Repeated violations after a prior warning (e.g., persistent off-topic posting after receipt of a Level 1 warning, continued sharing of assessment solutions after prior content removal)
  • Harassment or discriminatory behavior toward other users (e.g., targeted personal attacks, persistent unwanted contact, use of demeaning or degrading language in forum interactions)
  • Academic dishonesty or impersonation (e.g., submitting work attributed to another person, misrepresenting qualifications or credentials, using automated tools to complete assessments in a manner that violates the platform's academic integrity policy)
  • Spamming or persistent disruptive conduct (e.g., repeated posting of irrelevant or unsolicited content, automated or semi-automated posting that degrades the quality of community interactions)
  • Deliberate circumvention of content restrictions or temporary limitations imposed by moderators
  • Campaigns of coordinated disruptive behavior designed to undermine course instruction or community discourse

Duration of temporary suspensions typically ranges from 7 to 30 days depending on severity.

Level 4: Permanent Ban

Complete removal from the PyAcademy platform. All account data is retained as required by law but access is permanently revoked. A permanent ban is reserved for:

  • Severe violations such as threats, doxxing, or illegal activity (e.g., credible threats of violence, publishing another person's private identifying information without consent, any conduct that constitutes a criminal offense under applicable law)
  • Repeated violations after multiple prior penalties (e.g., a pattern of escalating misconduct despite progressive enforcement actions, demonstrating persistent disregard for community standards)
  • Creation of accounts to circumvent a prior suspension or ban (e.g., registering new accounts using alternate credentials to evade enforcement measures, assisting others in circumventing account restrictions)
  • Distribution of malware, phishing attempts, or security exploits (e.g., distributing malicious code, executing phishing campaigns targeting fellow learners, exploiting platform vulnerabilities for unauthorized access)
  • Content that sexualizes or exploits minors in any form
  • Conduct that poses an imminent threat to the safety of other users or the integrity of the platform's infrastructure
  • Systematic or organized activities designed to manipulate platform metrics, grades, or certification processes

4. Factors Considered in Determining Penalties

When determining the appropriate penalty, PyAcademy considers the following factors:

  • Severity of the violation: How harmful was the behavior to other users or the community?
  • Frequency: Is this a first offense or a repeated pattern of behavior?
  • Intent: Was the violation willful and deliberate, or accidental?
  • Impact: How many users were affected, and to what degree?
  • Cooperation: Did the user respond constructively when contacted about the issue?
  • Remediation: Has the user taken steps to correct the behavior?

In accordance with Article 14(4) of the DSA, where PyAcademy decides to impose a restriction on content, a suspension of access, or any other non-consensual measure, the statement of reasons provided to the affected user shall include, at a minimum: (a) the facts and circumstances relied upon, including a reference to the specific terms and conditions or legal provisions concerned; (b) the redress mechanisms available to the recipient of the service, including internal complaint-handling as provided for in Article 20 of the DSA and the right to an effective remedy before a court of law under Article 47 of the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union; and (c) the means, including the technology used, by which the provider identifies and assesses the content. Furthermore, pursuant to Article 15 of the DSA, PyAcademy shall provide clear, specific, and easily comprehensible notice to any affected user of any content moderation decision, and shall maintain and publish regular transparency reports detailing the scope, nature, and outcomes of its content moderation activities, including aggregate statistical data on notices received, decisions taken, and complaints processed.

6. Appeals Process

6.1. Right to Appeal

Every user has the right to appeal a moderation decision they believe was made in error. This right is established in accordance with Article 20 of the DSA, which requires hosting service providers to put in place effective, efficient, and transparent internal complaint-handling mechanisms to enable any recipient of their service to contest decisions related to measures taken on the basis of the provider's terms and conditions. Filing an appeal does not change your current penalty status while under review, except where the penalty would cause irreparable harm to the user's ability to access services already paid for, in which case a provisional stay may be considered pursuant to Article 20(2) of the DSA.

    6.3. Appeal Timeline

    PyAcademy will process appeals according to the following timeline:

    1. Acknowledgment: We will confirm receipt of your appeal within 2 business days
    2. Preliminary Assessment: Within 5 business days of receipt, the appeals team will conduct a preliminary assessment to determine whether the appeal falls within scope and contains sufficient information for a substantive review. If additional information is required, a request will be issued within this period with a deadline of 10 business days for the user's response.
    3. Review: A different member of the Trust & Safety team (not the person who made the original decision) will conduct a full, independent review of your appeal. This review shall include an examination of all evidence on file, the applicable terms and conditions, and the statements of reasons provided in accordance with Article 14(4) of the DSA.
    4. Decision: You will receive a written decision within 10 business days of submission. The decision shall set out the reasons for the outcome in accordance with Article 20(1) of the DSA, including the facts and circumstances established, the measures adopted, and the redress mechanisms available.
    5. Implementation: If your appeal is upheld, the penalty will be reversed within 3 business days of the decision, and any affected content will be restored to its original state. The reversal shall be recorded in the platform's moderation logs.

    Where PyAcademy is unable to reach a decision within the above timeframes, it shall notify the appellant in writing of the reasons for the delay and provide a revised timeline.

    6.4. Appeal Decisions

    After reviewing your appeal, PyAcademy may:

    • Uphold the original decision: The penalty stands as issued. The decision letter will set out the grounds on which the original decision is confirmed.
    • Reduce the penalty: The penalty is downgraded to a less severe tier (e.g., permanent ban reduced to temporary suspension). The reduced penalty shall reflect a reassessment of proportionality in light of the circumstances presented.
    • Reverse the decision: The penalty is fully removed from your account, and any associated restrictions on content or access shall be lifted immediately.

    The appeal decision is final. Filing multiple appeals for the same incident is not permitted. However, should new and material evidence come to light that was not available during the original appeal, you may submit a request for reconsideration within 30 calendar days of the appeal decision.

    • 社区成员使用"举报"按钮或电子邮件进行举报
    • 标记潜在有害内容的自动检测系统
    • PyAcademy 的审核团队在日常平台监督中进行的审查

    第一级：警告

    一份正式通知，告知您的行为违反了我们的社区公约，并且必须立即停止。警告记录在您的账户上，但不会限制您对任何功能的访问。警告通常针对以下情况发出：

    • 首次轻微违规（如发帖偏离主题、轻微的语言问题）
    • 无意违反社区标准（如意外发布重复内容、误用格式化工具）
    • 内容需要小幅调整以符合准则（如帖子缺少适当引用、讨论帖中不完整的代码片段）
    • 轻微违反讨论礼仪，如偏离帖文主题或发表与课程材料无关的评论
    • 无意发布虽无害但不符合平台学术诚信标准的信息
    第二级：内容删除

    违规内容从平台移除。您将收到通知，说明哪些内容被移除以及原因。内容删除通常针对以下情况：

    • 违反特定社区准则的帖子、评论或提交（如在错误的论坛板块发布内容、分享与平台学术诚信政策冲突的外部资源链接）

    • 包含不当、有害或不准确信息的内容（如可能损害用户计算环境的误导性技术指导、推广不安全编码实践的内容）
    • 过度自我推销或偏离主题的内容（如未经请求地推广外部服务、在同一课程论坛中多次重复发布相同的推广材料）
    • 未经授权再分发课程材料，包括直播课程录音、专有数据集或在限制性许可下分发的补充学习资源
    • 侵犯适用知识产权的内容，包括未经授权复制第三方受版权保护的材料
    第三级：临时暂停

    在规定期限内限制访问特定社区功能（如论坛、讨论或课程互动）。通常仍可访问课程内容和材料。临时暂停通常针对以下情况：

    • 在先前警告后重复违规（如在收到一级警告后持续发布偏离主题的内容、在先前内容删除后继续分享评估解决方案）
    • 对其他用户的骚扰或歧视行为（如有针对性的人身攻击、持续的不当联系、在论坛互动中使用贬损或侮辱性语言）

    • 蓄意破坏课程教学或社区讨论的协调性破坏行为

    完全从 PyAcademy 平台移除。所有账户数据将依法保留，但访问权限将被永久撤销。永久封禁保留用于：

    • 对其他用户安全或平台基础设施完整性构成迫在眉睫威胁的行为
    • 旨在操纵平台指标、成绩或认证流程的系统性或有组织活动

    4. 确定处罚时考虑的因素

    在确定适当的处罚时，PyAcademy 考虑以下因素：

    • 违规的严重程度：该行为对其他用户或社区造成了多大伤害？
    • 频率：这是初犯还是重复的行为模式？
    • 意图：违规是故意和蓄意的，还是无意的？

    • 配合度：当被联系讨论该问题时，用户是否做出了建设性的回应？
