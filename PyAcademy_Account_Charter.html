Account Charter

Effective Date: May 4, 2026

This Account Charter sets out the specific rules governing your PyAcademy account across its full lifecycle — from creation and day-to-day use, through tier transitions, to dormancy, transfer, and closure. It also governs use of the Online Judge system. It complements the User Agreement and Community Guidelines without repeating them.

Scope: This Charter applies to all PyAcademy accounts regardless of registration method, subscription tier, or geographic location. In the event of conflict with another policy, the more specific policy prevails for the topic it addresses; this Charter governs all account-lifecycle matters not addressed elsewhere.

1. Account Identity and Authenticity

1.1 One Real Person, One Primary Identity

Each PyAcademy account must be associated with a single, real individual. You may not create an account on behalf of a fictitious person, a collective identity, or an automated agent unless PyAcademy has granted you an explicit organisation or automation licence in writing. You are the sole authorised user of your account credentials; sharing login credentials with other individuals is prohibited regardless of their relationship to you.

1.2 Accuracy of Registration Information

The name, email address, and any other identifying information you provide during registration must be accurate and kept up to date. If your registration information changes materially — including a change of legal name or primary email address — you must update your account within 30 calendar days of the change. PyAcademy may suspend access to accounts that contain demonstrably inaccurate identity information until the information is corrected.

1.3 Display Names and Usernames

Your display name and username are public-facing identifiers visible to the PyAcademy community. You may not choose a display name or username that: impersonates another real individual, living or deceased; closely resembles a PyAcademy trademark, product, or staff title; consists solely of offensive, discriminatory, or harassing language; or was previously reclaimed by PyAcademy following a violation. PyAcademy reserves the right to require a username change at any time without compensation.

1.4 Profile Content Standards

Your profile avatar, bio, and linked URLs are subject to the same content standards as all other user-generated content under the Community Guidelines. Profile fields must not be used to advertise commercial services, solicit personal contact outside the platform, or circumvent content moderation by encoding prohibited material in profile images or text.

2. Account Tiers and Access Levels

PyAcademy offers several account tiers, each conferring different access rights and obligations.

Free

Free Account

Access to a curated subset of free courses, community forums, and the public OJ judge. Eligible for course completion certificates on free-tier enabled courses.

Pro

PyAcademy Pro

Full access to all course content, priority OJ queue, advanced skill badges, proficiency credentials, and enhanced community features. Governed additionally by the Pro Subscription Terms.

Organisation

Organisation Account

Enterprise licence for teams, companies, or bootcamps. Includes admin controls, bulk seat management, custom learning paths, and consolidated billing. Requires a separate Commercial Terms agreement.

Education

Education Account

Reduced-fee or grant-funded access for accredited educational institutions. Subject to annual re-verification of institutional status and restrictions on commercial use of outputs.

2.1 Tier Transitions

Upgrading takes effect immediately upon successful payment or verification. Downgrading takes effect at the end of the current billing period. If a downgrade causes you to exceed lower-tier limits, you will be notified and given 14 days to reduce usage before automatic archival of excess content occurs.

2.2 Eligibility Verification

Organisation and Education tiers require documentary verification of eligibility. PyAcademy may re-verify at any time. If a re-verification request is not responded to within 30 days, access may be downgraded to the Free tier pending completion. Providing false or misleading eligibility documentation constitutes a material breach of this Charter and may result in permanent account closure.

3. Authentication and Security Obligations

3.1 Authentication Methods

PyAcademy currently supports authentication through Auth0-managed identity providers. You are responsible for the security of any third-party identity provider account linked to your PyAcademy access. If a linked provider account is compromised, immediately revoke PyAcademy's access through that provider and notify us at security@li1234.top.

3.2 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

PyAcademy strongly recommends enabling MFA on any identity provider account linked to PyAcademy. For Organisation and Education tier accounts, MFA may be enforced by your administrator. PyAcademy will not be liable for unauthorised access that could have been prevented by enabling MFA.

3.3 Suspicious Activity and Incident Reporting

If you reasonably suspect your account has been accessed by an unauthorised party, you must: immediately change your identity provider credentials; revoke all active PyAcademy sessions via your account security settings; and notify PyAcademy at security@li1234.top within 24 hours. Failure to report a known compromise in a timely manner may affect your eligibility for account recovery assistance.

3.4 Session Management

You may maintain active sessions on multiple devices up to your tier's limit. PyAcademy may terminate sessions that are inactive for extended periods or originate from anomalous locations. Sign out of shared or public devices after each use; PyAcademy accepts no liability for information accessed via sessions left open on devices outside your control.

4. The Online Judge (OJ) System

4.1 Purpose and Scope

The PyAcademy Online Judge ("OJ") is an automated code evaluation system that compiles and executes user-submitted code against predefined test cases to assess correctness, efficiency, and style. Participation is subject to the rules in this Section 4, which apply in addition to all other applicable policies.

4.2 Permitted Use of the OJ

You may submit code to the OJ for: completing assigned course exercises; participating in sanctioned contests or hackathons hosted on PyAcademy; and personal practice on available problem sets. Each submission must represent your own work, or work clearly attributed to a permitted third-party source where the problem statement expressly allows it.

4.3 Prohibited OJ Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Exploiting the judge infrastructure: Submitting code designed to exploit or escape the sandboxed execution environment; consume disproportionate system resources (memory bombs, fork bombs, or equivalent); or interfere with other users' simultaneous submissions.
  • Harvesting test cases: Using timing-based attacks, differential analysis, or any other technique to reverse-engineer, infer, or extract private test case inputs or expected outputs not publicly disclosed.
  • Automating submissions at scale: Using scripts, bots, or any automated tooling to submit code without human review of each submission, except where PyAcademy has explicitly enabled automated CI/CD integration.
  • Contest violations: During timed contests — communicating solutions with other participants in real time; submitting solutions from external sources including AI assistants (unless contest rules explicitly permit it); or accessing the internet for solution lookup beyond what the contest environment provides.
  • Misrepresenting authorship: Submitting another person's solution as your own in contexts where individual authorship is evaluated, including course assessments affecting certification eligibility.

4.4 Submission Limits and Fair Use

Submission rate limits apply per account and per problem. If you require a higher rate for a legitimate automated integration (e.g., a classroom autograder), request an elevated quota in advance through the developer portal. Repeatedly triggering rate limits without a prior quota request may result in temporary OJ access suspension.

4.5 Code Execution Environment

Submitted code executes in an isolated sandbox. PyAcademy does not guarantee the environment is equivalent to any particular production runtime. OJ results are authoritative for scoring and certification purposes but should not be relied upon as definitive assessments of production-readiness. PyAcademy reserves the right to re-evaluate historical submissions if a systematic scoring error is discovered.

5. Account Reputation and Standing

5.1 Account Standing

Your account standing reflects the cumulative history of your adherence to PyAcademy's policies. Accounts in Good Standing have no active penalties. Accounts subject to active penalties are in Restricted Standing with limited access to specific features as described in the Account Penalties and Appeals Guide.

5.2 Restoration of Good Standing

Warnings expire automatically after 12 months if no further violations occur. Restrictions and suspensions expire at the end of the stated penalty period. Completion of a PyAcademy-assigned community standards module may, at PyAcademy's discretion, accelerate the expiry of a first-time warning or restriction. Good Standing is restored automatically upon expiry; no formal request is required.

6. Account Sharing, Delegation, and Teams

6.1 No Personal Account Sharing

Personal (Free and Pro) accounts are strictly single-user. You may not share login credentials or active sessions with any other person, including family members, colleagues, or students. Each individual who needs access must hold a separate account. Accounts found to be operated by multiple individuals may be suspended pending investigation.

6.2 Organisation Seat Management

Organisation accounts allocate named seats to individual members. Each seat may only be occupied by the assigned individual. Reassigning a seat is permitted through the admin panel and takes effect within 24 hours. The outgoing seat-holder's personal learning data is retained in their account; only their access to the organisation's private content is revoked.

6.3 Instructor and Moderator Roles

PyAcademy may invite specific users to elevated roles such as Course Instructor, Community Moderator, or Content Reviewer. These roles carry additional responsibilities set out in role-specific agreements. Role holders must not exercise elevated permissions for personal benefit, to circumvent enforcement actions against their own account, or on behalf of parties with undisclosed conflicts of interest.

7. Account Dormancy and Inactivity

7.1 Definition of Dormancy

An account is dormant if there has been no authenticated login for 12 continuous months (Free tier) or 24 continuous months (Pro, Organisation, or Education tier). Automated API calls using your credentials do not count as a login for dormancy purposes.

7.2 Dormancy Notifications

PyAcademy will send an email 60 days before dormancy classification and again 14 days before. Logging in at any point before the threshold resets the dormancy clock. If your registered email is no longer valid, PyAcademy may proceed with dormancy classification after making reasonable additional contact attempts.

7.3 Consequences of Dormancy

Dormant Free accounts may have their username released for reassignment after a further 6-month dormancy period, after which the account is archived. Archived accounts may be reactivated by contacting accounts@li1234.top within 2 years; after that window the account is permanently closed. Dormant Pro accounts are downgraded to Free tier at the next billing cycle following dormancy classification, but are not archived on the same timeline as long as Pro subscription payments continue.

8. Account Closure and Data Deletion

8.1 Voluntary Closure

You may close your account at any time through account settings or by emailing accounts@li1234.top. Voluntary closure initiates a 14-day grace period during which you may cancel the request. After that, your account enters a 30-day deletion queue. Following the queue period, your profile, learning history, and user-generated content are permanently deleted, except where retention is legally required or where content forms part of an irreversible community contribution (see Section 8.3).

8.2 Effect of Closure on Active Subscriptions

Closing your account while a Pro subscription is active cancels the subscription immediately. No refund is issued for the unused portion of the current billing period unless you are entitled to a statutory cooling-off refund under the Consumer Terms. Organisation and Education accounts may not be closed unilaterally by a member; closure must be initiated by the organisation administrator.

8.3 Irreversible Community Contributions

Certain contributions — accepted bug reports, forum posts cited in course materials, co-authored open OJ problems — may be retained in anonymised or attributed form after account closure, because removing them would degrade the experience of learners who relied upon them. Where your contribution is retained, your username will be replaced with "Former Member" or similar neutral attribution, unless you request full removal and PyAcademy determines removal does not disproportionately harm the community.

8.4 Certificates After Closure

Certificates issued before account closure remain accessible via their Verification URLs for the period stated in the Digital Certificate License. Verification will reflect your account status as "Closed" rather than "Active," but this does not automatically affect a certificate's validity. If your account was closed following a permanent ban for academic dishonesty, associated Certificates are revoked concurrently with closure.

9. Account Inheritance and Incapacity

9.1 No Automatic Inheritance

PyAcademy accounts are personal and non-transferable. There is no automatic mechanism for an account to pass to another individual upon the holder's death or legal incapacity. Certificates held by a deceased user remain accessible via Verification URLs as historical records but may not be transferred or re-issued in another person's name.

9.2 Authorised Representative Requests

A legally authorised representative (executor, administrator, or court-appointed guardian) may contact accounts@li1234.top to request: a download of the account holder's personal learning data; closure and data deletion; or memorialisation (marking the account as belonging to a deceased individual, disabling login while preserving public contributions). PyAcademy will require documentary evidence of authority before acting on any such request.

10. Cross-Account Conduct

10.1 No Circumvention via Alternate Accounts

You may not use a secondary account to perform actions blocked on your primary account — including participating in community features while suspended, submitting OJ solutions to contests from which you were disqualified, or contacting users who have blocked you. Using alternate accounts to circumvent enforcement is itself a violation that may result in all associated accounts being closed.

10.2 Linking Multiple Accounts

If you legitimately maintain more than one account (e.g., a personal account and an organisation seat), you must not use them to artificially inflate engagement metrics, obtain duplicate access to paid features, or exploit referral or incentive programmes beyond their intended single-use scope.

11. Notices and Communications

PyAcademy sends account-related notices (dormancy warnings, penalty notifications, policy updates) to your registered email address. You are responsible for keeping this address valid and monitoring it for PyAcademy communications. Unsubscribing from marketing emails does not unsubscribe you from transactional account notices. If a notice is sent and not responded to within the stated timeframe, PyAcademy may proceed with the notified action regardless of whether the email was opened.

12. Changes to This Charter

PyAcademy may update this Charter at any time. Material changes will be notified by email and via a prominent platform notice at least 30 days before taking effect. Continued use of your account after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the revised Charter. If you do not accept the revised Charter, you must close your account before the effective date.

13. Contact

Account lifecycle enquiries: accounts@li1234.top
Security incidents: security@li1234.top
OJ technical issues: oj@li1234.top

1. 账户身份与真实性

1.1 一人一账户原则

每个 PyAcademy 账户必须与单一真实个人关联。未经 PyAcademy 书面授予机构或自动化许可，您不得以虚构人物、集体身份或自动化代理的名义创建账户。您是账户凭证的唯一授权使用者，无论与他人的关系如何，均不得与他人共享登录凭证。

1.2 注册信息准确性

注册时提供的姓名、电子邮箱及其他身份信息必须准确，并保持最新。如您的注册信息发生实质性变更——包括法定姓名或主邮箱地址的变更——您必须在变更发生后 30 个日历日内更新账户信息。PyAcademy 可对包含明显不准确身份信息的账户暂停访问，直至信息得到更正。

1.3 昵称与用户名

您的昵称和用户名是向 PyAcademy 社区公开显示的标识符。您不得选择以下昵称或用户名：冒充真实个人（无论在世与否）；与 PyAcademy 商标、产品名称或员工职称高度相似；仅由冒犯性、歧视性或骚扰性语言构成；或曾因违规被 PyAcademy 收回的名称。PyAcademy 保留随时要求更改不符合条件的用户名的权利，且不予赔偿。

1.4 个人资料内容标准

您的头像、个人简介及关联 URL 与所有其他用户生成内容同样受《社区公约》内容标准约束。个人资料字段不得用于宣传商业服务、在平台外征集个人联系方式，或通过在头像或文本中编码禁止内容来规避内容审核。

3. 身份验证与安全义务

3.1 认证方式

PyAcademy 目前通过 Auth0 托管的身份提供商支持身份验证。您负责用于访问 PyAcademy 的任何第三方身份提供商账户的安全性。如关联的提供商账户遭到入侵，请立即通过该提供商撤销 PyAcademy 的访问权限，并通过 security@li1234.top 通知我们。

3.2 多因素认证（MFA）

PyAcademy 强烈建议在关联 PyAcademy 的任何身份提供商账户上启用 MFA。对于机构和教育等级账户，MFA 可能由管理员强制执行。对于本可通过启用 MFA 防止的未授权访问，PyAcademy 不承担责任。

3.3 可疑活动与事件报告

如果您合理怀疑账户被未授权方访问，您必须：立即更改身份提供商凭证；通过账户安全设置撤销所有活跃 PyAcademy 会话；并在 24 小时内通过 security@li1234.top 通知 PyAcademy。未能及时报告已知入侵可能影响您获得账户恢复协助的资格。

3.4 会话管理

您可以在多台设备上同时维持活跃会话，但不得超过等级适用的限额。PyAcademy 可终止长时间不活跃或来自异常位置的会话。使用完共享或公共设备后应退出登录；对于因您未控制的设备上遗留会话而被访问的信息，PyAcademy 不承担任何责任。

4. 在线判题系统（OJ）

4.1 目的与范围

PyAcademy 在线判题系统（"OJ"）是一套自动化代码评估系统，通过将用户提交的代码针对预定义测试用例进行编译和执行，评估其正确性、效率和代码风格。参与 OJ 系统须遵守本第 4 节的附加规则，这些规则在所有其他适用政策之上适用。

4.2 OJ 允许使用范围

您可以为以下目的向 OJ 提交代码：完成指定课程练习；参与 PyAcademy 举办的经认可的竞赛或黑客松；以及对可用题库进行个人练习。每次提交必须代表您本人的工作，或在题目说明明确允许时清晰归属于允许的第三方来源。

4.3 OJ 禁止行为

以下行为在使用 OJ 系统时被严格禁止：

  • 利用判题基础设施漏洞：提交旨在利用、逃脱或破坏沙盒执行环境的代码；消耗不成比例的系统资源（内存炸弹、fork 炸弹或等效手段）；或干扰其他用户的同时提交。
  • 采集测试用例：使用基于时序的攻击、差分分析或任何其他技术，对未公开的私有测试用例输入或预期输出进行逆向工程、推断或提取。
  • 大规模自动提交：使用脚本、机器人或任何自动化工具提交代码而不对每次提交进行人工审查，但 PyAcademy 明确启用自动化集成的情形除外。
  • 竞赛违规：在计时竞赛期间——与其他参与者实时交流解题方案；提交来自外部来源（包括 AI 助手）的解答（除非竞赛规则明确允许）；或超出竞赛环境通过互联网查找解答。
  • 伪造作者身份：在评估个人作者身份的情境下——包括影响证书资格的课程评估——将他人的解答作为自己的提交。

4.4 提交限制与公平使用
