BALEEN PLATE STRUCTURE

THE BALEEN FILTER

Engineering of nature's most efficient feeding system

STRUCTURE

300-400 keratin plates, each up to 3 meters long, with hair-like edges forming a precision filtration network.

FUNCTION

Each gulp swallows 500,000 liters of seawater, expelling water through the tongue while retaining krill with 99% efficiency.

EVOLUTION

Evolved from teeth 30 million years ago, adapting from hunting large prey to filtering tiny plankton.

STAGES OF DECOMPOSITION
01Mobile Scavengers (0-2 yrs)

Sharks, hagfish and large scavengers decompose soft tissue rapidly.

02Opportunists (2-5 yrs)

Polychaetes and crustaceans utilize remaining organic material.

03Chemosynthesis (5-50 yrs)

Bacteria break down bones producing hydrogen sulfide, sustaining tube worms.

04Reef Stage (50+ yrs)

Remaining skeleton becomes habitat and mineral source for deep-sea life.

How whales communicate across thousands of kilometers

SingingMale humpback complex songs for mating, traveling 100+ km, updating tunes yearly.
CommunicationShort pulses, whistles and moans for group coordination, frequency 20-10,000 Hz.
EcholocationToothed whales, high-frequency pulses (100-150 kHz) for navigation and hunting, precision to 1 cm.

GUARDIANS OF THE OCEAN

Whales as keystone species in marine ecosystems

NUTRIENT PUMP

Whale feces rich in iron and nitrogen promote phytoplankton growth. Each whale increases 10,000 tons of phytoplankton annually.

CARBON SINK

A whale stores 33 tons of carbon during its lifetime. After death, sinking equals the carbon sequestration of 1,000 trees.

VERTICAL MIXING

Deep diving and surfacing brings deep nutrients to the surface, promoting ocean vertical circulation.

TOP PREDATOR

Controlling prey populations, maintaining food chain balance, and promoting species diversity.
