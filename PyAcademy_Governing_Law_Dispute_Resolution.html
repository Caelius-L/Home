8. Limitation Periods and Prescription

8.1 General Prescription Period

Any claim or cause of action arising out of or relating to this Policy or the Applicable Agreements must be commenced within the applicable prescription (limitation) period under French law. Pursuant to Article 2224 of the French Code civil, the general prescription period for actions arising from a personal obligation or a movable property right is five (5) years from the date on which the claimant knew or ought to have known of the facts giving rise to the claim.

8.2 Specific Prescription Periods

The following specific prescription periods may apply under French law:

  • Contractual liability: Five (5) years from the date of the breach (Article 2224 of the Code civil).
  • Personal injury: Twenty (20) years from the date of the injury or the aggravation thereof (Article 2226 of the Code civil).
  • Product liability: Three (3) years from the date of discovery of the damage, the defect, and the identity of the manufacturer (Article 1142-6 of the Code civil, as amended by Loi n° 98-389 of 19 May 1998, transposing Directive 85/374/EEC).
  • Unfair commercial practices: Five (5) years from the cessation of the unlawful practice (Article L132-24 of the Code de la consommation).
  • Claims for payment of subscription fees: Two (2) years from the date the payment was due (Article L133-3 of the Code de la consommation for electronic payment instruments).

8.3 Suspension and Interruption of Prescription

The prescription period may be suspended (interrompu) or interrupted in accordance with the provisions of Articles 2240 et seq. of the French Code civil, including by means of:

  • Serving a formal notice (mise en demeure) or a commandement de payer.
  • Filing a lawsuit or initiating arbitration proceedings in good faith.
  • The debtor's recognition of the debt in writing.
  • The operation of force majeure in accordance with Article 2238 of the Code civil.

The operation of prescription is itself subject to the general rules of prescription under French law, including the rules applicable to the suspension and interruption thereof.
